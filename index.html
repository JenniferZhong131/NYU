Selected Research

Projects at the intersection of AI reliability and perception.

Central South University · May 2025 – Aug. 2025

W2R2: What–Where Representation Reforming for 3D Grounding

Identified a 2D semantic shortcut in multimodal 3D grounding using 2D-only ScanRefer diagnostics and t-SNE visualization, then proposed a pull–push training objective to encourage 3D features to dominate spatial grounding while 2D features retain semantic cues.

The project focuses on separating semantic “what” cues from spatial “where” grounding, improving geometric utilization without changing the inference-time architecture.

3D Grounding Multimodal Learning VLMs
New York University · Sep. 2025 – Jun. 2026

Reliable RAG for Spreadsheet Question Answering

Built a hybrid SQL–RAG pipeline for private spreadsheet QA, combining semantic retrieval with SQL verification to address retrieval incompleteness, metric ambiguity, and hidden assumptions in aggregation questions.

Evaluated direct answering, clarification-first answering, and dual-output multi-interpretation strategies on 25 ambiguous business questions and 75 total evaluations.

RAG SQL Verification Ambiguity-Aware QA
University of Liverpool · Aug. 2025 – Nov. 2025

T-MOTR: Temporal-Enhanced MOTR for Multi-Object Tracking

Developed a Transformer-based multi-object tracking extension for DanceTrack, integrating cross-attention gating, temporal self-attention, and temporal consistency loss to improve trajectory coherence under motion blur and occlusion.

Multi-Object Tracking Transformers DanceTrack
University of Liverpool · Dec. 2024 – May 2025

LipSwin: Lightweight Swin Transformer for Visual Speech Recognition

Implemented a Swin Transformer-based visual encoder for word- and sentence-level lip-reading on LRW, LRS2, and LRS3, with a 3D spatio-temporal embedding module and lightweight temporal attention.

Visual Speech Swin Transformer Video Understanding
